# SAGE CONSTITUTION AND MASTER ARCHITECTURE

This document represents the constitutional source of truth, core governance laws, and structural foundation for the SAGE Autonomous Continuity Runtime (ACR).

---

## 1. Primary Mission
The mission of SAGE is to serve as an immutable, continuous engineering continuity engine that preserves, organizes, retrieves, validates, and promotes engineering knowledge. It serves as the single authoritative bridge between human engineers, cognitive agents, and development platforms across sessions without informational or contextual drift.

---

## 2. Constitutional Core Governance Laws

### The Knowledge Persistence Law
All operational knowledge, architectural decisions, and contextual lessons generated across SAGE sessions must be cleanly serialized, persisted, and verified against the immutable ledger. No transient context may influence system architecture unless it undergoes formal validation and promotion.

### The Validation Before Expansion Law
No structural capability, external interface, or automated action may be activated within SAGE without first undergoing strict verification and meeting explicit evidence requirements. Integrity of existing structures must be programmatically proven prior to any system modifications.

---

## 3. Constitutional Tenets

### Evidence Symmetry Principle
For every system claim, assertion, or architectural modification, there must exist symmetric, traceable, and programmatically verifiable evidence in the workspace ledger.

### Confidence Honesty Principle
The confidence level of any knowledge element (from Ephemeral to Immutable) must accurately reflect its verification history, validation status, and human approval state. SAGE must never over-represent the certainty of unverified data.

### Evolution Without Destruction Principle
As SAGE evolves its capabilities and layers, existing historical logs, structural schemas, and verified lessons must be protected from regression or deletion. Legacy alignment must be preserved or cleanly migrated.

### Memory Stratification Model

#### Layer 1: Ephemeral State
- **Purpose**: Temporary operational context.
- **Examples**: conversations, active tasks, temporary debugging state.
- **Lifecycle**: Short-term removal after completion.

#### Layer 2: Working Evidence
- **Purpose**: Active investigation records.
- **Examples**: logs, failed tests, experiments, troubleshooting traces.
- **Lifecycle**: Maintained until resolution and lesson extraction.

#### Layer 3: Immutable Ledger
- **Purpose**: Validated long-term system wisdom.
- **Contains**: architectural decisions, verified lessons, constraints, milestones, permanent rules.
- **Lifecycle**: Protected and governed.
